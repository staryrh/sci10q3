NERVOUS SYSTEM

Central Nervous System

  • Brain

Brain

-receives and processes all of the information from all parts of the body.

Brain Control Center

Parts: Cerebrum, Cerebellum, Brain Stem

  • Cerebrum

    • -initiates and coordinates movement and regulates temperature.

  • Cerebellum

    • -responsible for balance, movement, and coordination.

  • Brain Stem

    • -regulate balance, breathing, heart rate and more.

Spinal Cord

-controls the entire organ system of the body

  • about 40-50cm
  • connects the brain and the body
  • composed of the gray and white body
  • transmits impulses all over the body to and from the brain
  • reponsible for involuntary movements (e.g. reflexes)

    • Autonomic

        Two TYpes of Autonomic

      • Sympathetic
        • Fight or Flight Response
        • Danger or stress activates your sympathetic nervous system.
      • Parasympathetic
        • Rest or Digest Response
        • Controls the internal functions at rest
